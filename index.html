Mapping Inequality: How Race & Income Impact COVID-19 Health Outcomes in Seattle, WA

Objective: Investigate how historical redlining, income disparity, and racial distribution correlate with COVID-19 health outcomes across Seattle using exploratory data analysis and geospatial mapping.

Methodology: Integrated Census and DOH datasets to create bivariate choropleth maps, analyzing spatial disparities in income, race, and healthcare access. Used Washington Tracking Network GIS tools and policy research to contextualize findings.

Impact: Presented a compelling, data-driven argument connecting socioeconomic inequality with public health outcomes. This visualization contributed to policy-focused discussions on equity in healthcare access during the COVID-19 pandemic.
